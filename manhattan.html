Manhattan Community Boathouse

A nonprofit organization that provides free kayaking during the summer right off Pier 96 on 56th street. They also offer free kayaking classes on specific days. The only requirement is knowing how to swim and being enthusiastic. To find out more click here.

The Highline

The High Line is a public park built on a historic freight rail line elevated above the streets on Manhattan. It's free to visit and you can imagine is quite unique, with exits and entrances to the street every few blocks. There are plenty of free cultural and cummunity events scheduled throughout the summer including Tai Chi classes, Latin Dance parties, movie screenings, and even stargazing. Find out more here.

Central Park

The most iconic park in NYC. There are many free activities but two stand out.

Summerstage runs from May through September. A great opportunity to see your favorite musical artists for free. Get more information here.

Plays by Shakespear are performed free at the Delacorte Theatre. The quality of actors talents are as good as any playing on Broadway. Tickets are distributed on day of performance at multiple locations. Find out more here.

NYC Parks

The NYC parks department has multiple recreational centers thoughout the boroughs of the city. The centers offer multiple activities and classes which include but are not limited to swimming lessons, movie screenings, fitness classes, and computer classes. Click here for more info.
